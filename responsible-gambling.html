What is Responsible Gambling?

Responsible gambling means:

  • Understanding that gambling is entertainment, not a source of income
  • Playing within your means and setting strict limits
  • Recognizing the signs of problem gambling early

Signs of Problem Gambling

Recognizing warning signs early can help you or a loved one seek support. Problem gambling may include:

  • Constant thoughts about gambling or past gambling sessions
  • Needing to gamble with increasing amounts of money for excitement
  • Unsuccessful attempts to control, cut back, or stop gambling
  • Gambling as an escape from problems or emotional distress
  • Lying to family and friends about gambling habits
  • Risking relationships, jobs, or educational opportunities due to gambling
  • Borrowing money or going into debt to gamble
  • Feeling desperate about financial losses and attempting to "win it back"
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms (irritability, anxiety) when not gambling

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone else, please reach out to one of the support organizations listed above.

  • Never chase losses: Accept losses and walk away rather than trying to "win it back"

  • Don't gamble under the influence: Avoid playing after drinking alcohol or using substances

  • Talk to someone: Share your gambling activities with trusted friends or family
